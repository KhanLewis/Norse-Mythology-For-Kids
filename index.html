What is Norse Mythology?

"Norse" is a term for the medieval Norwegian or Scandinavian civilisation. “Mythology” is a collection of myths partically related to religious or cultural tradition. So… Norse mythology is medieval Norwegian myths! The time of the Norse, also known as the Viking age! Was said to be around (c. 790- c. 1100 CE). The Vikings believed that bravery in battle was a way to reach Valhalla. They believed if they died in battle winged female spirits called Valkyries would fly down and guide their souls to back to Odin to where he lived, Valhalla!

Norse Gods

Odin

The King of all the Gods as well as the God of Battle. Odin's name means furious one in the Old Norse language. The Vikings used to think that he caused battles on Earth by hurling his powerful spear which was called Gungnir. He would through it to Midgard (which is Earth in Norse myths) to cause these battles! Odin also rode an eight-legged horse called Sleipnir. Odin had two ravens called Hugin and Munin. These ravens would fly around Midgard, collectiing information for the Kind of all gods!

Freyja

Freya was the goddess of love and beauty. She wept golden tears when sad. Wearing a cloak made from falcon feathers, her chariot was pulled through the sky by two cats. But she would also ride her faithful boar, Hildisvíni.

Thor

Odin’s burly son Thor was the god of physical strength and thunder – he was the common people’s favourite! Thor made lightning by throwing his magic hammer, called Mjollnir. Viking men wore a charm of Thor’s hammer around their necks for good luck. They believed that claps of thunder were the sound of his chariot’s wheels! His chariot was pulled by giant goats!

MUSEUM

A distaff found in the Lendbreen pass.

“This is called a distaff it is made from birch wood and is around 1200 years old. Archaeologists found it at Lendbreen. The archaeologists also said they has found a similar distaff in the Oseberg Viking ship burial. This tool is used to spin wool. “

A tinderbox found in the Lendbreen pass.

“The tinderbox contained a wooden stick and small bits of resin-filled wood. Archaeologists believe that it is from the Viking Age or the Medieval Period (500 to 1000 years old), though it could be younger or older.”

The Lendbreen tunic, which dates to the year 300, is the oldest piece of clothing ever found in Norway.

“The tunic is made from wool, which was spun and woven. It survived because it has been inside the ice for most of time since it was lost. It is believed that the tunic could have been left behind due to the final stages of hypothermia being very warm, leading to the person taking it off.”
